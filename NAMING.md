# Naming

**Brand / line:** **Calcumaker**.
**This device:** **Calcumaker 16** — the programmer's / technical RPN calculator.
**Tagline:** *"Every base. Every digit."* (alt: *"RPN, to arbitrary precision."*)

## Why "16"

- **Hexadecimal heritage.** The defining trait is the **HP-16C** lineage — a
  *programmer's* calculator: hex / octal / binary / decimal, bitwise and
  shift/rotate ops, selectable word sizes. "16" reads immediately as **base-16 /
  hex** to the target audience.
- **Honors the lineage without borrowing the wordmark.** It evokes the HP-16C
  spirit while "Calcumaker 16" is its own name (see trademark posture).
- **Leaves room for a product line.** A future variant could be a "Calcumaker
  10" (decimal/scientific-leaning) or similar — "16" makes this unit's identity
  specific rather than generic.

## Repo vs product

- The **repo** stays `calcumaker` (the brand/line).
- The **product** is **Calcumaker 16**.
- The **two PCBs** are `calcumaker-main` (MCU/PSU/keypad) and
  `calcumaker-display` (the angled 7-seg stack board). Schematic title blocks
  read "Calcumaker 16 — Main" / "Calcumaker 16 — Display".

## Trademark posture (informal — not legal clearance)

Deliberately avoided as wordmarks: **HP**, **HP-16C**, **Voyager** (HP/HPE) —
referenced only as a *category/lineage* descriptor, not as branding. Before any
public launch, run a proper USPTO/TESS + EUIPO search (ideally via a trademark
attorney), and re-verify domain availability.

## Alternatives kept in pocket

- **Calcumaker Hex** — more explicit, less product-line-friendly.
- **Calcumaker RPN** — describes the input model but not the programmer angle.
- **CM-16** — terse hardware-style designator; usable as a silk/label short form.
